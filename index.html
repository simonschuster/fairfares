Helpful travel tips

What every traveler — and reviewer — should know

The most common mistakes that lead to reimbursement disputes, explained plainly.

Always get a comp at the time of booking
G-28 requires a cost comparison at or before the time of booking — not after. Use Connexxus or a UC travel agency. "I couldn't find one" is not an acceptable explanation. FairFares exists precisely for cases where the comp was missed.
UCOP G-28
The 24-hour arrival rule is not flexible
You may arrive up to 24 hours before your first meeting. Up to 48 hours requires exceptional pre-approval. If you arrive earlier for personal reasons — vacation, sightseeing, visiting family — UC pays only the equivalent of what the correct departure date would have cost. The same rule applies in reverse on departure.
UCOP G-28
Seat class — keep it simple and non-refundable
Book basic economy or economy extra (extra legroom within the economy cabin). Never book a refundable fare — they are not reimbursable under any circumstances. If your plans change, a change fee is almost always cheaper than paying a premium for a refundable ticket. Premium Economy (a separate cabin) requires pre-approval. Business class requires pre-approval and one of four specific circumstances. First class is never allowable.
UCOP G-28
Rental car vs taxi — do the full maths
A rental car is only justified if it is genuinely cheaper than a taxi or rideshare. That means rental cost plus gas plus parking plus tolls — compared against the taxi fare for the exact same journey. In most city trips the taxi wins. If you chose a rental car and it was not cheaper, UC reimburses the taxi equivalent only. Use the Ground Transport tab to get the metered taxi fare for your route.
UCOP G-28
Extended or indirect travel — show every extra cost
If you claim it was cheaper to leave a day later, travel via a connection, or take an indirect route, you must show the complete cost comparison. That means the fare saving minus every extra cost incurred — additional hotel nights, meals for extra days, ground transport. The net saving must be real, documented, and calculated at the time of booking. Partial comparisons will not be accepted.
UCOP G-28
Conference hotel above $333 — attach the agenda
If the conference hotel rate exceeds the $333/night domestic cap, you may still stay there without exceptional approval — as long as you attach the conference agenda or flyer showing the hotel and prearranged rate. No further justification is needed. For all other hotels above $333, price comparisons obtained at time of booking are required.
UCOP G-28
International travel — per diem means no receipts needed
For international and OCONUS travel (including Hawaii and Alaska), DoD per diem rates apply and can be treated as a flat daily allowance — no meal receipts required up to the rate. Lodging is capped at 300% of the DoD lodging rate. Use the International tab to look up rates for your destination. Domestic travel is different — actuals only up to $92/day, not a per diem.
UCOP G-28 / DoD
Grant funding does not override UC policy
Even if a grant explicitly permits business class travel, first class, or other non-standard expenses, UCOP G-28 still applies. Grant permission does not override university policy. If an expense is not normally reimbursable, it remains non-reimbursable regardless of the funding source.
UCOP G-28

International per diem — how it works

What is a per diem? A per diem is a fixed daily allowance that covers meals and incidental expenses (M&IE). For international and OCONUS travel (including Hawaii and Alaska), UC uses DoD per diem rates. The key benefit: travelers can claim up to the per diem rate without providing meal receipts — it is treated as a flat daily allowance.

International vs domestic — a critical difference. Domestic US travel is NOT a per diem. It is capped at $92/day but requires actual receipts and cannot be treated as a flat allowance. International travel IS a per diem — travelers claim up to the DoD rate with no receipts needed.

Lodging cap for international travel. International hotel reimbursement is capped at 300% of the DoD lodging rate for that city. Up to 200% requires department approval. Over 200% up to 300% requires Dean's Office or equivalent approval. Above 300% is never approved.
