Helpful travel tips

What every traveler — and reviewer — should know

The most common mistakes that lead to reimbursement disputes, explained plainly.

G-28 requires a cost comparison at or before the time of booking — not after. Use Connexxus or a UC travel agency. "I couldn't find one" is not an acceptable explanation. FairFares exists precisely for cases where the comp was missed — but it is always better to get one at the time of booking.
Travelers may arrive up to 24 hours before their first meeting. Up to 48 hours requires exceptional pre-approval. If they arrive earlier for personal reasons — vacation, sightseeing, visiting family — UC pays only the equivalent of the correct departure date. The same applies on departure: leave on the last day of the meeting or the day after if more economical or involves an overnight flight. Personal days at the end are at the traveler's own cost.
Book basic economy or economy extra (extra legroom within the economy cabin). Never book a refundable fare — they are not reimbursable under any circumstances. If plans change, a change fee is almost always cheaper than a refundable ticket. Premium Economy (a separate cabin) requires pre-approval. Business class requires pre-approval and one of four specific circumstances only. First class is never allowable under any circumstances.
A rental car is only justified if it is genuinely cheaper than a taxi or rideshare. That means rental cost plus gas plus parking plus tolls — compared against the taxi fare for the same journey. In most city trips the taxi wins. If a rental car was chosen and it was not cheaper, UC reimburses the taxi equivalent only. Use the Ground Transport tab to get the metered taxi fare for any route.
If claiming it was cheaper to leave a day later, travel via a connection, or take an indirect route, you must show the complete cost comparison. That means the fare saving minus every extra cost incurred — additional hotel nights, meals for extra days, ground transport. The net saving must be real, documented, and calculated at the time of booking. Partial comparisons will not be accepted.
If the conference hotel rate exceeds the $333/night domestic cap, the traveler may still stay there without exceptional approval — as long as they attach the conference agenda or flyer showing the hotel and prearranged rate. No further justification is needed for this exception. For all other hotels above $333, price comparisons within proximity of the meeting location are required.
For international and OCONUS travel (including Hawaii and Alaska), DoD per diem rates apply and can be treated as a flat daily allowance — no meal receipts required up to the rate. Lodging is capped at 300% of the DoD lodging rate. Use the International tab to look up rates for your destination. Domestic travel is different — actuals only up to $92/day, never a per diem.
Even if a grant explicitly permits business class travel, first class, or other non-standard expenses, UCOP G-28 still applies. Grant permission does not override university policy. If an expense is not normally reimbursable, it remains non-reimbursable regardless of the funding source. This applies to all fund types including NIH, NSF, and private grants.

International per diem — how it works

What is a per diem? A per diem is a fixed daily allowance that covers meals and incidental expenses (M&IE). For international and OCONUS travel (including Hawaii and Alaska), UC uses DoD per diem rates. The key benefit: travelers can claim up to the per diem rate without providing meal receipts — it is treated as a flat daily allowance.

International vs domestic — a critical difference. Domestic US travel is NOT a per diem. It is capped at $92/day but requires actual receipts and cannot be treated as a flat allowance. International travel IS a per diem — travelers claim up to the DoD rate with no receipts needed.

Lodging cap for international travel. International hotel reimbursement is capped at 300% of the DoD lodging rate for that city. Up to 200% requires department approval. Over 200% up to 300% requires Dean's Office or equivalent approval. Above 300% is never approved.
